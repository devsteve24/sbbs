Welcome to the Book of Jude

Explore Jude 1:17-25 with insights and questions to reflect upon.

Jude 1:17–25

17 But you, beloved, remember the words which were spoken before by the apostles of our Lord Jesus Christ:

18 how they told you that there would be mockers in the last time who would walk according to their own ungodly lusts.

19 These are sensual persons, who cause divisions, not having the Spirit.

20 But you, beloved, building yourselves up on your most holy faith, praying in the Holy Spirit,

21 keep yourselves in the love of God, looking for the mercy of our Lord Jesus Christ unto eternal life.

22 And on some have compassion, making a distinction;

23 but others save with fear, pulling them out of the fire, hating even the garment defiled by the flesh.

24 Now to Him who is able to keep you from stumbling, And to present you faultless Before the presence of His glory with exceeding joy,

25 To God our Savior, Who alone is wise, Be glory and majesty, Dominion and power, Both now and forever. Amen.

Jude 17–19 – Remember Apostolic Warnings

17 But you, beloved, remember the words which were spoken before by the apostles of our Lord Jesus Christ:

18 how they told you that there would be mockers in the last time who would walk according to their own ungodly lusts.

19 These are sensual persons, who cause divisions, not having the Spirit.

Key Verse

“Remember the words spoken by the apostles.”

Main Points

  • Mockers were predicted, following ungodly desires.
  • Characteristics: self-centered, divisive, devoid of the Spirit.

Application

Be vigilant and grounded in Scripture.

Discussion Questions

Message Questions:
  • Why did the apostles warn about mockers?
  • What traits of mockers stand out most?
Personal Questions:
  • How do you respond to mockery or ungodly influences?
  • How can these warnings strengthen your faith?

Jude 20–21 – Build and Keep Your Faith

20 But you, beloved, building yourselves up on your most holy faith, praying in the Holy Spirit,

21 keep yourselves in the love of God, looking for the mercy of our Lord Jesus Christ unto eternal life.

Key Verses

“Build yourselves up… praying in the Holy Spirit.”

Main Points

  • Build faith through Scripture, prayer, and fellowship.
  • Pray in alignment with the Holy Spirit.
  • Keep yourselves in God’s love through obedience.
  • Look for Christ’s mercy and eternal hope.

Application

Actively grow in faith and live with eternity in mind.

Discussion Questions

Message Questions:
  • What does it mean to build your faith?
  • How does praying in the Spirit help?
Personal Questions:
  • What can you do this week to grow in faith?
  • How do you stay in God’s love during challenges?

Jude 22–23 – Reach Out to Others

22 And on some have compassion, making a distinction;

23 but others save with fear, pulling them out of the fire, hating even the garment defiled by the flesh.

Key Verses

“Have compassion… save others, pulling them out of the fire.”

Main Points

  • Show compassion to those doubting.
  • Rescue others urgently from sin.
  • Avoid being influenced by their sin.

Application

Balance compassion with caution while helping others.

Discussion Questions

Message Questions:
  • Why does Jude urge compassion for some and urgency for others?
  • What does “snatching from the fire” mean?
Personal Questions:
  • Who needs your compassion or help this week?
  • How can you protect yourself while helping others in sin?

Jude 24–25 – Trust in God’s Power

24 Now to Him who is able to keep you from stumbling,

And to present you faultless Before the presence of His glory with exceeding joy,

25 To God our Savior, Who alone is wise, Be glory and majesty, Dominion and power, Both now and forever. Amen.

Key Verses

“Now to Him who is able to keep you from stumbling.”

Main Points

  • God sustains and preserves believers.
  • Believers are presented faultless through Christ.
  • All glory, majesty, and dominion belong to God.

Application

Rest in God’s ability to help you finish strong and praise Him.

Discussion Questions

Message Questions:
  • What does it mean that God keeps us?
  • How does Jude’s doxology reflect God’s glory?
Personal Questions:
  • When has God helped you persevere?
  • Where do you need to trust God more today?

Summary and Application

Summary

  • Jude urges us to reject false teachings, grow in faith, help others, and trust God.
  • Key actions: Stay vigilant, persevere, and encourage others.

Application

  • Live your faith daily through Scripture, prayer, and action.
  • Support others with compassion and urgency in their spiritual walk.

Conclusion

Message Questions:
  • Why is contending for faith essential today?
  • How does trusting God’s power give hope?
Personal Questions:
  • What step will you take to strengthen your faith this week?
  • Who can you encourage or guide spiritually?
